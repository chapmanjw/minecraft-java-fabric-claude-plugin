# Room catalog

Rooms a player base may contain, grouped by purpose. Each entry gives a
**minimum useful interior** (W×D×H) and the must-haves. Footprints are
interior — add wall thickness. Keep any single room within ~60×60×60 so it
fits one structure file. Offer rooms filtered by tier — do not propose a
beacon room to a starter shack.

## Living

- **Entryway / foyer** — 4×4×4. Door, light, a mat; tool/food station within
  2 blocks of the door. The first impression of the base.
- **Bedroom** — 5×5×4. Bed (set spawn), light, a chest, a window. Master
  bedroom 7×7+ with seating and an ender chest.
- **Kitchen** — 5×5×4. Furnace/smoker, food storage (barrels), a table,
  water source. A campfire-on-hay chimney reads well.
- **Dining room** — 6×6×4. Table, chairs, lighting; adjacency to kitchen.
- **Living / great room** — 8×8×5. Seating, fireplace (campfire vented up),
  display, generous glazing. The social hub.
- **Bathroom** — 4×4×3. Cosmetic — cauldron tub, item-frame "sink", carpet.
- **Guest room** — 5×5×4. Bed, chest, light. Beds forbidden in nether/end.
- **Panic room** — 4×4×3. Hidden, sealed, a bed, a food chest, an escape
  shaft. Counts as the second exit.

## Utility

- **Storage room / vault** — sized to the storage scheme (see
  `interiors.md`). Categorized barrels or double-chest banks; item frames as
  labels.
- **Crafting room** — 5×5×4. Crafting table(s), 1.21 crafter for automation,
  fletching/cartography/smithing tables, stonecutter, grindstone.
- **Smelting array** — a row of hopper-fed furnaces/smokers/blast furnaces
  with a fuel feed; see `utilities.md`.
- **Enchanting room** — 7×7×4 to seat the **15-bookshelf** ring: bookshelves
  exactly 2 blocks (laterally) from the table, same level or one above, with
  the 2-high gap kept clear (a torch, carpet, or snow cover in the gap kills
  that shelf). 15 active shelves give the level-30 ceiling.
- **Brewing room** — 5×5×4. A row of brewing stands, ingredient barrels
  colour-coded, a water/cauldron source.
- **Anvil / repair station** — 3×3×3. Anvil, grindstone, an XP store nearby.

## Display

- **Trophy hall** — long room; item frames, pedestals (slabs/walls), lighting.
- **Armor gallery** — armor stands in a lit row; consider posed stands.
- **Library** — bookshelves (decorative — keep clear of the enchanting ring),
  reading nooks, lecterns, chiseled-bookshelf storage.
- **Map room / war room** — a wall of item-framed maps; a central table.
- **Throne room / music room / aquarium** — themed showcase spaces, tier
  estate and up.

## Specialty

- **Greenhouse** — glazed, lit, planters; doubles as a small crop supply.
- **Animal pens / stable / aviary** — fenced, lit, water and feed; a boat dock
  for water access.
- **Observatory** — a glass-domed top room with a spyglass and open sky.
- **Workshop / forge** — crafting-heavy themed room.
- **Wardrobe / treasury / sauna / pool** — comfort and luxury rooms, estate+.

## Infrastructure

- **Nether portal room** — obsidian frame, contained, **no bed**. Decorative
  blackstone/crying-obsidian surround.
- **End portal room** — a 12-eye frame in a sealed shrine, **no bed**.
- **Beacon room** — a 4-tier pyramid is **164 mineral blocks**
  (81+49+25+9), with a clear vertical line of sight to the sky. Iron blocks
  are the cheap option.
- **Conduit room** — a prismarine / sea-lantern frame; a **16-block minimum**
  frame activates it, the full 42-block frame maxes the range. Slabs, stairs,
  and walls do not count toward the frame.
- **Farm interface rooms** — the player-facing collection point for an iron
  farm, mob farm, or crop farm; the farm itself is a `utilities.md` build.
- **Mining-tunnel entry / sleeping bunker** — practical access spaces.

## Sizing rules

- Reject a bedroom under 4×4×3, a kitchen under 3×3×3, or any room that cannot
  fit its required furniture.
- Every interior space ≥4 tall unless the user asks for a crawlspace.
- A "blank canvas" room (the user will furnish it themselves) still needs
  walls, a floor, a ceiling, light, and a door.
