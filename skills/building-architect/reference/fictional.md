# Fictional buildings

Two kinds: **canonical** buildings from a named work, and **generative** ones
in a named style. Both must still read as detailed, deliberate architecture.

## Canonical replicas — resolve the adaptation first

A famous fictional building usually has **several incompatible versions** —
book, film, game, theme-park. They differ in silhouette, scale, and detail.
**Ask the user which adaptation to follow before designing**, and cite it.

- **Hogwarts** — the book describes a seven-storey castle; the films grew a
  distinct silhouette (the Astronomy Tower is a film-era addition); the
  theme-park version uses forced perspective. They are not the same building.
  Hogwarts is **deliberately asymmetric** — a symmetric reproduction is wrong.
- **Minas Tirith** — book vs Jackson films vs the Amazon series differ. At
  lore scale it is a seven-tier city ~200 m tall; a true 1:1 height strains
  the world Y range — scale the height down, keep the footprint.

For canonical builds: get the silhouette, the tier/tower list, and the
signature features from the `researcher`; cite the wiki or primary source;
record the adaptation in `requirements.md`.

Representative targets and their signatures:

- **Hogwarts Castle** — many towers of varied height, the Great Hall, the
  viaduct and boathouse, a rocky lakeside promontory. Asymmetric. Palette:
  blended stone / cobblestone / andesite, mossy cobble, deepslate tile,
  spruce and dark oak, copper finials.
- **Minas Tirith** — seven white concentric tiers, the rock prow splitting
  each tier, the White Tower crowning the citadel. Palette: calcite + smooth
  quartz, deepslate-tile trim.
- **Rivendell** — cascading terraces in a river gorge, waterfalls, a narrow
  stone bridge, hex/oct pavilion roofs with finials. Needs `terraforming` to
  carve the valley. Palette: oak/birch timber, calcite, leaves and vines.
- **Helm's Deep** — a curtain wall across a gorge, the Hornburg keep, a
  causeway. A fortress — lean on the curtain-wall and tower modules.
- **A fairy-tale castle** (Disney/Cinderella style) — slender towers, steep
  blue conical roofs, gold finials. Symmetric, idealized.

## Generative fictional styles

When the user asks for something "in the style of X" with no specific
building, compose from a **named style**. Each style is a palette ratio, a set
of signature shapes, a decoration vocabulary, and a lighting mood.

- **Elven** — 60% diorite/calcite/quartz, 20% birch/spruce log, 10% gold
  trim, 10% leaves/vines. Pointed arches, asymmetric organic curves,
  cascading terraces, hex/oct towers. Soft hidden lighting, soul fire.
- **Dwarven** — 50% deepslate/polished deepslate, 25% smooth stone, 15% gold
  block, 10% iron. Rigid symmetry, massive columns, hex halls. Lava channels
  behind iron bars; lantern and lava light.
- **Steampunk** — 40% copper (full oxidation range), smooth stone, iron,
  spruce, gold trim. Gears, pipes (dripstone), copper bulbs. Copper bulbs dim
  as they oxidize — a built-in lighting gradient.
- **Cyberpunk** — deepslate and black concrete, neon from coloured glow
  blocks and end rods, stained-glass accents. Hard edges, towering arcologies.
- **Gothic fantasy** — blackstone, deepslate, dark prismarine, stained glass;
  pointed arches, ribbed spires, gargoyle accents, dramatic verticality.
- **Greco-Roman classical** — smooth quartz, calcite, sandstone; colonnades,
  pediments, domes, strict symmetry.
- **Egyptian fantasy** — sandstone family, gold and lapis (blue) accents;
  battered pylon walls, obelisks, hypostyle halls.
- **Norse / Viking** — spruce, stone, carved posts, turf roofs; longhouses,
  steep gables, dragon-prow detailing.
- **Samurai / Edo** — dark wood framing, white plaster, blue or weathered-
  copper tiered roofs with upturned eaves.
- **Persian / Arabian** — sandstone and glazed terracotta, onion domes,
  horseshoe arches, geometric tilework.
- **Wizard / arcane** — crooked tall towers, amethyst and copper, glowing
  whimsy, improbable cantilevers.
- **Brutalist fantasy** — massive bare concrete/deepslate masses, deep
  recesses, monumental and severe.

For any generative build, ask the **mood** — pristine, lived-in, ruined, or
under-construction — and apply weathering accordingly (see `techniques.md`).
