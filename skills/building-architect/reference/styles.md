# Real-world architectural styles

Era reference for real-world replicas and period-correct originals. Each style:
its era, the defining features, the Minecraft material translation, and the
characteristic shapes. Getting the era right prevents the "period mismatch"
failure (a medieval castle with a glass curtain wall).

## Ancient

- **Egyptian** — battered (tapered) walls, pylons, obelisks, hypostyle halls
  of massive columns. Sandstone family; gold and blue (lapis) accents.
- **Mesopotamian** — stepped ziggurats, mud-brick mass. Terracotta, packed
  mud, sandstone.
- **Greek** — post-and-lintel temples, fluted columns (Doric/Ionic/Corinthian
  capitals), pediments, strict symmetry. Smooth quartz, calcite, white wool.
- **Roman** — the arch, the vault, the dome; aqueducts, amphitheatres,
  basilicas. Smooth stone, sandstone, brick; concrete look via stone.

## Medieval

- **Byzantine** — pendentive domes, half-domes, rich mosaic. Smooth sandstone,
  calcite, gold block, blue terracotta.
- **Romanesque** — round arches, thick walls, small windows, barrel vaults,
  sturdy towers. Stone brick, cobblestone, mossy variants.
- **Gothic** — pointed arches, ribbed vaults, flying buttresses, soaring
  verticality, huge stained-glass windows and rose windows. Smooth stone,
  polished diorite, deepslate-tile tracery, stained glass.

## Early modern

- **Renaissance** — revived classical symmetry, domes, pilasters, harmonic
  proportion. Smooth stone, quartz, sandstone trim.
- **Baroque** — dramatic curves, lavish ornament, grand stairs, gilding.
- **Rococo** — lighter, ornate, pastel Baroque.
- **Neoclassical** — restrained columned classicism; porticoes, domes
  (government buildings, memorials). Quartz, calcite, smooth stone.

## 19th century

- **Georgian / Federal** — symmetric brick, sash windows, restrained trim.
- **Victorian / Gothic Revival** — eclectic, ornate, asymmetric massing,
  decorative trim, polychrome brick.
- **Beaux-Arts** — grand, symmetric, heavily ornamented classicism.
- **Art Nouveau** — organic flowing lines, nature motifs, ironwork.

## 20th century onward

- **Art Deco** — setback profiles, vertical emphasis, geometric ornament,
  metallic crowns. Smooth sandstone, smooth stone, iron and gold accents.
- **International Style / Bauhaus** — glass curtain walls, flat roofs, no
  ornament, steel frame. Glass, iron bars, quartz, concrete.
- **Brutalism** — massive raw concrete, deep recesses, monumental. Smooth
  stone, light gray concrete, deepslate.
- **Contemporary / parametric** — sweeping curves, unconventional geometry,
  glass and metal (Guggenheim Bilbao, Sydney Opera House).

## Non-Western traditions

- **Japanese** — timber frames, white plaster, tiered roofs with upturned
  eaves, verandas. Dark wood, smooth stone, blue or copper roofs.
- **Chinese** — timber halls on stone plinths, sweeping tiled roofs, red
  columns, glazed-tile roofs. Spruce, red terracotta, yellow/green glazed
  terracotta.
- **Islamic** — domes, minarets, horseshoe and pointed arches, geometric
  tilework, courtyards. Sandstone, glazed terracotta, calcite.
- **Indian** — ornate temple shikhara towers, carved mass, stepped profiles.
- **Mesoamerican** — stepped stone pyramids, broad stairs, carved relief.
  Stone brick, mossy and cracked variants, sandstone.
