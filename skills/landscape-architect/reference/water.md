# Water features

Water is the centerpiece of most designed gardens — reflecting it, channeling
it, terracing it. You design the **static** water; animated water (dancing
fountains, water organs) is an `engineer` handoff.

## Reflecting pools

A still rectangle or geometric basin whose job is to mirror the sky and the
architecture (a parterre d'eau, a Mughal canal, a Lincoln-Memorial-style pool).

- Use **water source blocks** throughout — no flowing water — so the surface
  is dead still and mirror-flat.
- Proportion the rectangle 1:3 to 1:8 for a canal-like pool; keep the geometry
  exact and aligned to the axis.
- Make it **3–5 blocks deep** so the reflection reads, and place sea lanterns
  (or glowstone) under the floor so the water is bright and clear, not murky.
- Edge it with a crisp stone coping.

## Fountain basins

The static basin and pool of a fountain — circular, octagonal, square, or
lobed. Tiered basins step down in shrinking rings. You design the basin
geometry, the water-source layout, and the coping; the **figure** on the
fountain is a `monument-builder` handoff, and any **animated jets** are an
`engineer` handoff.

## Channels (Mughal acequia)

Narrow straight water channels, 1–2 blocks wide, running along the axes of a
char bagh. Built of source blocks for a still channel; for a deliberate flow,
a gentle 1-block-per-stretch slope with a source block every few blocks. Edge
with stone; punctuate with small jets at the crossings.

## Water staircases

The Italian-Renaissance cascade — a series of small stepped basins descending
a terraced slope, water spilling from each to the next. Each step is a small
source-block basin with a spill lip. The terracing itself is a `terraforming`
pre-build step; you design the basins on it.

## Designed lakes

The English-landscape lake — *looks* natural but its perimeter is a deliberate
composed curve (a long sinuous shape that reveals and conceals as you walk the
circuit), not a freehand wobble and not a geometric pool. The lake basin is a
`terraforming` job; you give it the **exact designed shoreline curve** and the
placement of the temples, bridges, and tree clumps around the circuit.

## Bedrock notes

- Still water = source blocks everywhere; the moment a tile is flowing water
  the surface ripples and the reflection breaks.
- Light the floor (sea lanterns / glowstone beneath) for clear, bright water.
- Keep a basin within the structure and fill caps like any other element;
  large reflecting pools tile on their symmetry centerline.
- Water cannot sit in the Nether; not a concern for a normal garden, but flag
  it if a garden is ever planned there.
