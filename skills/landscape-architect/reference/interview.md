# The adaptive interview

Pin down the garden without over-asking. Ask in small grouped batches (use
`AskUserQuestion` for the multiple-choice ones), skip what is already settled
by the request or a delegating skill, and record answers in `requirements.md`.

## Question bank

1. **Tradition** — French formal, Italian terraced, English landscape, English
   Arts-and-Crafts, Mughal char bagh, Islamic courtyard, Japanese stroll,
   Japanese zen, Japanese tea, Chinese scholar, modernist, contemporary
   perennial, civic plaza, or monastic cloister? Allow a deliberate fusion.
2. **Scale** — a courtyard or zen garden (small, 1:1), a private garden, a
   chateau's or villa's grounds, or a royal park (large — built at ~1:4)?
3. **Site context** — standalone with its own envelope, or a delegated
   envelope (from `city-planner` for a plaza/park, from `building-architect` /
   `player-house` for the grounds of a building)?
4. **Adjacent building** — what does the garden sit against, and where is its
   main axis / door? A formal garden extends the building's centerline.
5. **Features** — which to include: parterres, a hedge maze, knot gardens,
   topiary (which shapes), allées, bosquets, fountains, reflecting pools, water
   channels or staircases, a designed lake, a kitchen garden, a rose garden,
   pergolas, gazebos or follies, statuary, sundials, belvederes.
6. **Roofed structures** — any orangery, summerhouse, temple, or tea house?
   (Each is a `building-architect` handoff; you place the footprint.)
7. **Statuary** — any figures or sculptural fountains? (A `monument-builder`
   handoff.)
8. **Terrain prep** — does the site need leveling, a raised belvedere hill, or
   a terraced slope? (A `terraforming` handoff.)
9. **Project name** — the registry slug.

## Conditional follow-ups

- A named garden or unfamiliar tradition → invoke `researcher` for plans and
  dimensions.
- A large garden (Tier 2+) → confirm the reduced scale (~1:4) and that the
  proportions stay exact.
- A hedge maze → confirm path width, hedge height, and the center reward.
- A garden meeting wild terrain → ask for the transition apron treatment.
- Animated water wanted → note the `engineer` handoff for the jets; you design
  the static basin.
- A Japanese stroll garden → there is no axis — gather the sequence of framed
  viewpoints instead.

## Conduct

- Lead with the tradition and scale — they govern the geometry.
- Group questions; never interrogate line by line.
- For a delegated envelope, honour the given rectangle, datum, and axis — do
  not renegotiate them.
- Restate the design — tradition, scale, axes, feature list — before rendering
  blueprints, so a misread is cheap to fix.
